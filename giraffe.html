GIRAFFE

A giraffe is the tallest land animal in the world, known for its incredibly long neck and legs. It’s a herbivorous mammal native to Africa and belongs to the genus Giraffa.

🦒 Key Facts About Giraffes: 📏 Physical Features: Height: Adult males can reach up to 5.5–6 meters (18–20 feet) tall. Neck: Extremely long, with 7 vertebrae (same as humans, just much longer). Horns: Called ossicones – short, skin-covered bone structures on the head. Coat: Patchy spots that vary by subspecies, used for camouflage and heat regulation. 🌍 Where They Live: Native to sub-Saharan Africa. Commonly found in: Savannas Woodlands Open plains Examples of countries: Kenya, Tanzania, South Africa, Botswana, Namibia 🌿 Diet: Herbivores – eat mostly leaves, fruits, and flowers. Prefer acacia trees. Long tongues (about 45 cm / 18 in) help them grab foliage. 👨‍👩‍👧 Behavior: Usually found in loose social groups. Males sometimes engage in “necking” (swinging their necks to fight over mates). Sleep very little – often only 10–30 minutes a day in short naps! 🦁 Predators: Mainly preyed on by lions, especially the young or weak. Use strong kicks to defend themselves. ❗ Conservation Status: Some subspecies are vulnerable or endangered due to: Habitat loss Poaching Protected in many reserves and parks. You can find giraffes in the savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands of sub-Saharan Africa. They live in areas where their favorite trees, especially acacias, are plentiful.
